Over the course of several weeks, I embarked on a project to learn Unreal Engine 5 by recreating the core gameplay of Fall Guys in a solo effort.
This exercise was designed to explore the engine’s capabilities while building a fun and dynamic gameplay experience inspired by the chaotic charm of the original game.

The project focused on delivering key elements of Fall Guys, including:

Trap and obstacle mechanics, such as spinning bars, falling platforms, and moving blocks, to challenge the player.
A player-controlled character with physics-based movement and animations, ensuring a playful, responsive feel.
Multiple maps, each with unique layouts and challenges, to keep gameplay fresh and engaging.
The objective was to create a functional and entertaining prototype while mastering key Unreal Engine 5 tools and workflows.

As the sole developer, I:

Designed and implemented trap and obstacle systems, ensuring they interacted smoothly with player physics.
Built a player character system with movement, jumping, and ragdoll-like physics to capture the chaotic fun of Fall Guys.
Created maps with distinct challenges, leveraging Unreal Engine 5’s powerful level design tools to make environments visually and mechanically engaging.

This project allowed me to dive deep into Unreal Engine 5, gaining hands-on experience with Blueprints, physics systems, and level design.
By recreating the energetic and unpredictable gameplay of Fall Guys, I also improved my ability to balance mechanics for fun and fairness.
