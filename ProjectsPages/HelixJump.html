In a challenging yet rewarding school exercise, my task was to recreate the core gameplay of Helix Jump within a strict three-hour time limit.
This project focused on delivering the essence of the game: dynamic platform navigation combined with engaging, physics-based mechanics.

The recreated game included:

Rotatable platforms forming a vertical tower with gaps for the ball to fall through.
A player-controlled bouncing ball, responding to gravity and collisions.
Fail states when the ball hits certain platform zones, adding risk and intensity to the gameplay.
The aim was to replicate the core mechanics of Helix Jump while optimizing for time and simplicity, ensuring a functional prototype by the end of the session.

In this time-constrained project, I focused on:

Implementing platform rotation, allowing smooth and responsive player input for navigating the ball.
Physics-based ball movement, simulating realistic bounces and interactions with the tower.
Adding a basic level progression system, creating an engaging flow as the player descends.

This exercise was a testament to the importance of rapid prototyping and prioritizing essential features under pressure.
