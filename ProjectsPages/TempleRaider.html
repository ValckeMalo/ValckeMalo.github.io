Over the course of several weeks, I undertook a project to create a puzzle game using Unreal Engine 5, combining C++ and Blueprints to implement the core gameplay mechanics.
This exercise was designed to deepen my understanding of Unreal's scripting systems while crafting a game that emphasized logic and creativity.

The puzzle game centered around mechanics such as:

Interactive objects, like levers, pressure plates, and movable blocks, which players could manipulate to solve challenges.
Dynamic level mechanics, where player actions directly altered the environment, unlocking new paths or activating mechanisms.
A system for progression tracking, ensuring puzzles became more challenging as the player advanced through levels.
The focus was on creating an engaging, logic-driven experience that highlighted both technical and design skills.

In this solo project, I worked on:

Core gameplay mechanics in C++, such as player-object interactions and game logic, ensuring efficiency and flexibility.
Leveraging Blueprints to rapidly prototype and iterate on puzzles, fine-tuning gameplay and testing new ideas.
Designing and implementing puzzle levels, crafting scenarios that required logical thinking and experimentation to solve.

This project allowed me to blend C++ and Blueprint workflows, providing valuable insights into the strengths of each system.
It also honed my ability to design puzzles that balance challenge and accessibility, ensuring an engaging experience for players.
