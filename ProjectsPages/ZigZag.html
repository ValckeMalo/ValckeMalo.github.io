In another fast-paced school exercise, the challenge was to recreate the core gameplay of ZigZag in Unity within a three-hour time limit.
The project emphasized agility, precision, and the ability to distill a game into its essential mechanics.

The recreated game included:

A zigzagging player-controlled ball, dynamically moving forward along a narrow path.
Responsive input to change direction at each corner, challenging timing and reflexes.
A system of procedurally generated paths, ensuring the gameplay remains engaging and unpredictable.
The objective was to capture the essence of ZigZag with a focus on smooth controls and infinite playability.

In this exercise, I worked on:

Ball movement and direction switching, ensuring responsive and satisfying controls for the player.
Path generation, creating an endless sequence of randomized turns while maintaining a playable difficulty curve.
Implementing collision mechanics, adding fail states when the ball falls off the path.

This project demonstrated the importance of prioritizing core gameplay elements under time constraints.
